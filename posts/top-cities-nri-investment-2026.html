Top 5 Cities NRIs Are Betting On in 2026

5 min read·VittaBridge Advisory·Market Insights

India's residential real estate market has seen strong NRI interest over the past three years, driven by a combination of favourable exchange rates, infrastructure momentum in key cities, and a post-pandemic reassessment of the value of owning property in one's home country.

Not all cities are equal, however. NRI investment tends to concentrate in markets that offer the right combination of liquidity, infrastructure, employment fundamentals and long-term capital appreciation potential. Here are the five cities that dominate NRI investment conversations in 2026 — and why.

1
Our Top Pick · 2026

Bengaluru

India's technology capital continues to lead NRI investment for good reason. The city's fundamentals remain stronger than any other Indian market: a deep employment base anchored by global technology companies, consistent rental demand from a young professional workforce, and a real estate market that has demonstrated resilience through multiple economic cycles.

In 2026, the north and east corridors — Whitefield, Sarjapur Road, Hebbal, Devanahalli — remain most active for NRI buyers. Infrastructure investments including Namma Metro Phase 3 expansions and the Peripheral Ring Road are unlocking new micro-markets. Bengaluru also has a disproportionately high share of bank auction properties relative to other metros, making it particularly interesting for VittaBridge clients pursuing the auction route. For NRIs with a 5–10 year horizon, Bengaluru offers the most compelling risk-adjusted return profile of any Indian city.

2
High Appreciation · Strong Rental Yield

Hyderabad

Hyderabad has arguably been India's most consistent real estate story of the past decade. The city's HYDRAA governance framework, its relative affordability compared to Mumbai and Bengaluru, and its emergence as a major hub for global capability centres have created sustained demand. The western suburbs — HITEC City, Gachibowli, Kokapet, Financial District — remain the core NRI investment zones. For buyers seeking higher ticket sizes with strong corporate rental demand, Hyderabad is difficult to ignore.

3
Best Rental Yields · Young Demographics

Pune

Pune's combination of IT/ITeS employment, manufacturing sector growth (now reinforced by electric vehicle and semiconductor investments) and a large student population creates one of India's most stable rental markets. For NRI investors focused on yield rather than pure appreciation, Pune's mid-segment residential market offers returns that are harder to replicate in Mumbai or Bengaluru. Hinjewadi, Baner, Wakad and Kharadi are the consistently strong NRI-focus corridors.

4
Undervalued · Strong Infrastructure Pipeline

Chennai

Chennai is frequently underestimated by NRI investors focused on the Bengaluru–Hyderabad–Pune trio. The city's manufacturing and automobile sector depth, its port-driven logistics economy and its emerging data centre ecosystem provide a diversified economic foundation that reduces concentration risk. Property prices in Chennai's OMR (Old Mahabalipuram Road) corridor and the emerging north Chennai industrial belt offer value that is harder to find in more celebrated markets. Chennai also benefits from a strong Tamil diaspora in the US, UK, Singapore and Malaysia — creating a natural NRI buyer base.

5
Fastest Growing · Infrastructure Momentum

Ahmedabad

Ahmedabad's inclusion reflects a broader shift in NRI investment patterns. The GIFT City (Gujarat International Finance Tec-City) development has put Ahmedabad on the international investment map, attracting global financial services companies and creating a new class of high-income residents. Beyond GIFT City, the Sarkhej–Gandhinagar Highway corridor and the SG Highway micro-market have seen sustained appreciation. For NRIs with a Gujarati background or family connections, Ahmedabad offers a particularly compelling entry point while valuations remain accessible compared to western metros.

The right city depends on your personal objectives — whether that's rental income, capital appreciation, future self-use, or a combination. There is no universally correct answer. What matters is matching the city's fundamentals to your investment horizon and risk profile.

What About Mumbai?

Mumbai's absence from this list is deliberate. While it remains India's financial capital and commands premium valuations, the sheer entry cost — combined with lower rental yields relative to price — makes it a less efficient use of NRI investment capital compared to the five cities above. For NRIs with strong personal or family connections to Mumbai, it remains relevant. For pure investment logic, the cities above offer better returns for the capital deployed.

The Auction Angle

All five cities have active bank auction markets. Bengaluru and Hyderabad in particular see significant auction activity given the scale of their mortgage markets. NRIs willing to navigate the auction process — with proper advisory support — can acquire properties in all five cities at meaningful discounts to the open market prices referenced above.
